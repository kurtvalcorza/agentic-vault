# Provocation Bank: Quick Reference

Use these provocations to stimulate thinking during content creation. Copy and adapt as needed.

---

## Universal Provocations

### Before Starting
- "What's the one thing I want readers to remember?"
- "Who's my skeptic, and what would they say?"
- "If I knew the answer already, would I still write this?"
- "What would make this piece unnecessary?"

### During Writing
- "What am I implicitly assuming here?"
- "What am I choosing NOT to say? Is that intentional?"
- "Who disagrees with this claim, and why?"
- "What's the strongest counterargument?"

### Before Finishing
- "If I had to cut this in half, what would go?"
- "What question will the first reader ask?"
- "In six months, will I stand by this?"
- "If I'm wrong, what are the consequences?"

## By Writing Phase

### Exploration (Understanding)
| Use When | Provocation |
|----------|-------------|
| Unclear direction | "What's the core tension here?" |
| Too many ideas | "If I could only make ONE point, what is it?" |
| Stuck starting | "Who cares about this, and why?" |
| Overwhelmed by sources | "What gap do none of these fill?" |

### Development (Building)
| Use When | Provocation |
|----------|-------------|
| Making a claim | "What evidence would prove me wrong?" |
| Synthesizing | "How do these pieces connect—or do they?" |
| Citing sources | "Does this source actually say what I think it says?" |
| Adding section | "Does this earn its space?" |

### Refinement (Polishing)
| Use When | Provocation |
|----------|-------------|
| Hedging | "What am I actually claiming?" |
| Long paragraph | "Can I say this in half the words?" |
| Uncertain tone | "Would [alternative tone] serve my goal better?" |
| Ready to send | "What would the harshest critic say?" |

## By Content Type

### News Releases
```
STORY
- "Achievement, change, challenge, or vision—which story?"
- "What's the sentence that gets quoted?"
- "If a competitor read this, what would they think?"

AUDIENCE
- "Journalists want X, investors want Y—am I serving both?"
- "What's the one thing each audience takes away?"

DATA
- "What's the tension in these numbers?"
- "If the news were bad, would I frame it this way?"

QUOTES
- "Does this sound like [executive] or like marketing?"
- "What function does this quote serve?"
```

### Academic Writing
```
LITERATURE
- "Am I citing this because it's relevant or because it exists?"
- "What would falsify this paper's claims?"
- "Where does this disagree with others I've read?"

ARGUMENT
- "Can I draw a map from known → unknown → my contribution?"
- "Does every paper I cite actually support what I claim?"
- "What's missing from my synthesis?"

CLAIMS
- "A reviewer will ask about [X]. What's my response?"
- "Am I disagreeing based on evidence or preference?"
- "What's my actual confidence level?"
```

### Technical Reports
```
CONTEXT
- "What decision will someone make based on this?"
- "What would change that decision?"

DATA
- "What's the weirdest thing in this data? Why?"
- "What doesn't this metric capture?"
- "Compared to what benchmark?"

INTERPRETATION
- "What's the alternative explanation?"
- "Does my language match my confidence?"
- "So what—what should someone DO with this?"

RECOMMENDATIONS
- "Can I trace the logic from findings to recommendation?"
- "What would make me change this recommendation?"
- "If I'm wrong, was I wrong or unlucky?"
```

### Speaking Engagements (Talking Points, Remarks, Keynotes)

```text
COMPRESSION
- "What's the ONE message if you only had 30 seconds?"
- "You have 3 key points. Which one would you defend under hostile questioning?"
- "Your compressed cues say X, but your backup paragraph says Y. Which is your real position?"

AUDIENCE
- "Your audience includes [skeptics]. What's their strongest objection?"
- "What's the follow-up question you're most afraid of?"

DELIVERY
- "If the moderator interrupts you here, can you recover your point?"
- "You've allocated equal time to all points. Which actually matters most?"
- "The Q&A will focus on [topic]. Is your script ready for that pivot?"

COMMITMENT
- "This sounds like a commitment. Can you deliver on it?"
- "What backstop do you have if someone asks for specifics?"
```

## Conversation Starters

When you want to engage in "Tools for Thought" mode:

**To start exploration:**
> "Before I write anything, help me think through the tensions in this topic."

**To challenge your draft:**
> "What's weak in my argument? Where would a skeptic attack?"

**To test framing:**
> "I'm framing this as [X]. What would [Y] framing look like? What are the tradeoffs?"

**To find gaps:**
> "What obvious question am I not addressing?"

**To strengthen claims:**
> "My main claim is [X]. What would I need to prove it?"

**To refine tone:**
> "I wrote this with [X] tone. Would [Y] tone serve my purpose better?"

---

## The Meta-Provocation

When stuck at any stage:

> "What would I need to know to proceed confidently?"

This question surfaces your actual uncertainty and points toward what you need to resolve.

---

## Anti-Patterns: What NOT to Ask AI

| Don't Ask | Ask Instead |
|-----------|-------------|
| "Write a draft for me" | "What are the tensions I need to resolve?" |
| "Summarize these papers" | "What lens should I read these through?" |
| "What should my conclusion be?" | "What conclusions does my evidence support?" |
| "Is this good?" | "Where would a skeptic attack this?" |
| "Fix this for me" | "What's unclear or weak here?" |

## Quick Decision Framework

**When making any writing decision, ask:**

1. **Emphasis**: What does this choice emphasize? De-emphasize?
2. **Audience**: Who benefits from this choice? Who doesn't?
3. **Tradeoff**: What am I giving up by choosing this?
4. **Reversal**: What would the opposite choice look like?
5. **Defense**: Can I explain why I made this choice?

If you can answer all five, you've thought it through.
