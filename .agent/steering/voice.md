## Voice (House Style)

> ✏️ **Customize this file.** The *principles* below are general-purpose; the *examples* use a fictional placeholder — "Atlas," an in-house AI platform. Replace them with phrasing, numbers, and audiences from your own work so agents can hear your house voice.

Most professional writing is, in effect, ghostwritten — for a team, an organization, or a principal (an exec, a project lead). So "voice" here means your *output* voice, not a personal-diary voice. If you keep a communication-framework skill, use it to calibrate structure to audience; the traits below hold across all of it. What to avoid is in [[anti-style]].

### Core traits

- **Impact-first.** Lead with what changed for people, then the mechanism. *"Teams ship a model in an afternoon — down from the week it used to take,"* not *"we refactored the deployment pipeline."*
- **Number-anchored.** Specific figures over adjectives — *"onboarded 40 teams," "cut inference cost 38%."* The numbers are the credibility.
- **Plain-language translation.** Every technical concept gets a one-line human gloss. Pass the Grandmother Test: could a non-expert repeat it?
- **Active, owned "we."** *"We're turning Atlas into a self-serve platform."* Present-tense, accountable.
- **Before/after framing.** Scattered scripts → one shared pipeline. Manual review → automated checks.
- **Declarative rhythm, em-dash appositives.** Short, factual sentences. Em-dashes to define inline — *"Atlas — the shared workbench for building, testing, and deploying models."*
- **Confident, not promotional.** State the result; don't sell it. Earn the closer: *"Year one proved the platform works. Year two is where we scale it."*

### Audience calibration

> ✏️ Replace these frames with your real audiences.

| Audience | Frame |
|---|---|
| Press / media | "Solving a problem most people don't know exists" — human interest, before/after |
| Leadership | "Advances the strategy" — alignment, scale, return |
| Technical peers | "A new capability you can build on" — credibility, access |
| Customers / users | "What you can now do without extra effort" — practical, low-barrier |
| General public | "Real people, real benefit" — relatable, concrete |

### Where this applies

Default for `01_Projects/`, `02_Areas/`, `03_Resources/`, and `.agent/outputs/`. `Inbox/` and personal notes stay casual; session logs stay neutral/factual.
