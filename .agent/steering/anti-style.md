## Anti-Style

The negative space of your voice. A lot of credibility comes from what you refuse to write. Applies to all substantial and external-facing outputs (`01_Projects/`, `02_Areas/`, `03_Resources/`, `.agent/outputs/`). The positive counterpart is [[voice]].

### Banned vocabulary

**Hype / marketing-speak** — reads as empty, erodes credibility:
- groundbreaking, revolutionary, game-changing, world-class, cutting-edge, state-of-the-art, next-generation, paradigm shift, best-in-class
- Report results plainly and let the numbers carry the weight.

**AI-tells** — instantly reads as machine-written:
- "delve into," "in today's fast-paced world," "it's important to note," "stands as a testament to," "plays a pivotal/crucial role," "navigate the landscape," "in the realm of," "harness the power of"
- "underscore," "showcase," "robust," "seamless" used as filler intensifiers

**Empty intensifiers** — cut, or replace with the figure:
- "significantly / dramatically / vastly" → give the number ("up 38%," "minutes to seconds")

> ✏️ **Keep an allowlist.** Some words that look like buzzwords are legitimate domain/mission vocabulary in your field — don't let the anti-style flag them. List yours here. *Example for a platform team: enable, ship, self-serve, pipeline.*

### Structural anti-patterns

- **Burying the lead** — the impact goes in sentence one, not after three paragraphs of background.
- **Implementation-first** — say what changed for people, then how. *"Teams can ship without ops support,"* not *"we deployed containerized workflows."*
- **Unexplained jargon** — every acronym or technical term gets a plain-language gloss on first use (*Atlas — our shared model workbench*).
- **Fabricated specificity** — never invent a number or metric. *"Early results show promise"* if the data isn't final.
- **Hedging** — say it or cut it; no "it could be argued that."
- Numbered lists where a sharp paragraph would do.

### Boundaries

> ✏️ Adapt to your context. Common ones:

- Don't overpromise or imply a timeline you can't hold.
- Figures must match the source — never round up for effect.
- Attribute partners and collaborators correctly. Credit is currency.
- Keep a consistent register for public-facing work; match any house or brand guidelines.

### Pre-send test

Before declaring a draft done, ask:

1. Does the impact land in the first sentence?
2. Did a hype word or AI-tell slip in?
3. Is every number traceable to a source?
4. Would a skeptic catch something you de-emphasized?
5. Will this still be accurate six months from now?
